About MakeCode Arcade

Microsoft MakeCode Arcade is a free, open source retro 2D game development environment. Students anywhere from 9 to 18 can use MakeCode Arcade to design and create their own games online that they can then share with friends and family or download to hand-held game devices. Learn more about Microsoft MakeCode at http://aka.ms/makecode.

Activity Requirements

The MakeCode Arcade Hour of Code™ activities require students to have a computer with internet access. All activities run in the browser – no download or installation required.

Learning Outcomes

  1. “Asphodel Follows Directions” game – students are challenged to pick up and move directional arrows through a maze to guide Asphodel through to reach her trophy. Students learn decomposition and sequencing concepts, trial and error, and begin to familiarize themselves with the MakeCode Arcade game environment.
  2. Design Activity – students can express their creativity and artistic talent by making their own game characters and backgrounds. Students learn that games are a great way to combine code and art to create a unique digital artifact that others can play.
  3. Shark Attack tutorial – following a step-by-step tutorial, students will code their own game. Students will learn computer science concepts such as variables, events, random functions, and kinds. In addition, students will learn game development concepts such as sprites, projectiles, position and velocity, and game loop.

Tips for Facilitation

These MakeCode Arcade activities are meant to be self-guided. However, you can encourage students to do the following:
  • Help each other if they get stuck
  • Click on the "Hint" button in any tutorial
  • Share the games they create with others to play. Note that this generates a URL link that can be copied
  • If they finish early, try some of the suggested additional activities
  • You can also choose to participate as a class in a MakeCode Arcade Game Jam
