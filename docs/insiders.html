What is MakeCode?

Microsoft MakeCode is a free, open-source code platform that allows users to write programs for a wide variety of applications even if  they have little or no previous coding experience.

Program Overview

Through the MakeCode Insiders Program, teens will have the opportunity to engage with a select group of peers, while directly influencing the future development of MakeCode.  Insiders will share and build upon their knowledge of coding through weekly sessions with members of the MakeCode team. In these sessions, they will complete tutorials and provide feedback with their cohort of 10-15 fellow Insiders.  Extra “at-home” activities will also be assigned over the course of 10-12 weeks.  Insiders will benefit by connecting with likeminded peers, making connections at Microsoft, building coding knowledge, and earning recognition for completing key  program objectives.

  

Program Criteria

To be eligible for the program, teens must meet the following criteria.

  • Teens must be 13-17 years old (born between March 2003 and December 2008)
  • Live in the United States
  • Read and speak English fluently
  • Be available for an hour-long virtual session every week for three months
  • Be available to dedicate up to two hours of additional time each week to Insider projects
  • No coding experience required
