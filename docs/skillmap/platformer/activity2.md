## Start Tile 

ðŸŽ¥ Now we can move around and the camera will follow ðŸŽ¥  

Unfortunately, our player is starting in dangerous position!

By default, all sprites spawn in the middle of the screen.
Let's change the game so your player starts somewhere safe.
<hr/>

ðŸ”² Snap a ``||scene:place [mySprite] on top of random [ ]||`` block 
into the bottom of the ``||loops:on start||`` container.

ðŸ”² Click the checkerboard tile and replace it with the green flag.
<hr/>
**Now your sprite starts on the green flag tile!**  

>> *Tip: If you want your sprite to start somewhere else, 
you can move the green flag by editing the
[__tilemap__](#tilemp "a grid of square tiles that makes up our background").

## Game Over Pt. 1

ðŸ’€ Time to add some *danger* to this game ðŸ’€  

When the player overlaps the skull tile, we'll trigger a "GAME OVER".
<hr/>

ðŸ”² Drag the ``||scene:on [sprite] of kind [Player] overlaps [ ] at [location]||`` container 
into the workspace.

ðŸ”² Click the checkerboard image and change it to the skull tile.

ðŸ”² Snap a ``||game:game over <LOSE>||`` block into the new container.
<br/>

```blocks
scene.onOverlapTile(SpriteKind.Player, myTiles.tile2, function (sprite, location) {
    game.over(false)
})
```

## You Win Pt. 1

ðŸŽˆ We have a way to lose, let's add a way to win ðŸŽˆ  
<hr/>

ðŸ”² Drag another ``||scene:on [sprite] of kind [Player] overlaps [ ] at [location]||`` 
container into the workspace.

ðŸ”² This time, change the checkerboard to the trophy tile.

ðŸ”² Snap a ``||game:game over LOSE>||`` block into the new container and toggle 
the &lt;LOSE&gt; switch to &lt;WIN&gt;!
<br/>

```blocks
scene.onOverlapTile(SpriteKind.Player, myTiles.tile4, function (sprite, location) {
    game.over(true)
})
```
